Among the destinations we cover, a handpicked selection includes

Abbey falls

Abbey Falls, nestled near Madikeri in Karnataka's Kodagu district, is a breathtaking cascade renowned for its scenic beauty. Plunging from a height of approximately 70 feet (21 meters), the falls create a captivating sight as they descend into a tranquil pool below. Surrounded by verdant forests and coffee plantations, Abbey Falls epitomizes the pristine allure of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Accessible via a picturesque trek through lush foliage, visitors can immerse themselves in the serenity of nature along the trail. Facilities such as parking, viewpoints, and local vendors add convenience to the experience, ensuring visitors can savor the tranquility and capture unforgettable moments. While the monsoon season, from June to September, showcases the falls at their fullest, visitors are encouraged to practice responsible tourism to preserve the natural beauty and biodiversity of Abbey Falls for future generations to relish. A visit to Abbey Falls promises not only a visual feast but also a soul-soothing retreat, offering a respite from the bustle of everyday life amidst the serene embrace of nature.

Irrpu falls

Irpu Falls, situated in the Coorg district of Karnataka, India, stands as a magnificent testament to the region's natural splendor. Flowing gracefully amidst the verdant landscapes of the Western Ghats, Irpu Falls cascades down a height of approximately 170 feet (52 meters), creating a mesmerizing spectacle of cascading water. Surrounded by lush forests and diverse flora, the falls exude a serene ambiance that beckons visitors seeking solace in nature's embrace. Accessible via a scenic trek through the Brahmagiri Wildlife Sanctuary, the journey to Irpu Falls offers glimpses of the region's rich biodiversity, including unique plant and bird species. Visitors can bask in the tranquility of the falls' surroundings, immersing themselves in the sights and sounds of the wilderness. Facilities such as parking areas, viewpoints, and refreshment stalls enhance the visitor experience, ensuring comfort and convenience. While the monsoon season, from June to September, showcases the falls in their full glory, visitors are urged to respect the environment and practice responsible tourism to preserve Irpu Falls' natural beauty for future generations to enjoy. A visit to Irpu Falls promises not only a visual feast but also an enriching journey through the heart of Coorg's pristine wilderness, leaving indelible memories of its majestic grandeur.

Dubare Elephant Camp

Dubare Elephant Camp, situated on the banks of the Kaveri River amidst the lush forests of Coorg, offers a unique opportunity to interact with majestic elephants in their natural habitat. Managed by the Karnataka Forest Department, the camp is a haven for elephant lovers and conservation enthusiasts alike. Visitors can participate in a range of activities, from bathing and feeding the elephants to learning about their behavior and habitat. The camp also offers nature walks, boat rides, and birdwatching opportunities, making it an ideal destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers of all ages

Raja's seat

Raja's Seat, a scenic viewpoint nestled amidst the rolling hills of Madikeri, is a place of unparalleled beauty and tranquility. Legend has it that the kings of Coorg would come here to enjoy the mesmerizing sunset views, surrounded by lush gardens and colorful flowers. Today, visitors flock to Raja's Seat to witness the breathtaking spectacle of the setting sun casting its golden glow over the Western Ghats. The gentle breeze, the fragrance of blossoms, and the panoramic vistas make Raja's Seat a must-visit destination for anyone seeking solace in nature's embrace

Nagarahole Tiger Reserve

Nagarahole National Park, nestled in the picturesque landscapes of Karnataka, India, stands as a sanctuary of biodiversity and natural beauty. Spread across the districts of Kodagu and Mysore, this pristine wilderness is part of the larger Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, renowned for its rich flora and fauna. The name "Nagarahole" translates to "Snake River" in Kannada, derived from the meandering course of the river that flows through the park. Spanning over 600 square kilometers, Nagarahole is home to a diverse range of habitats, including dense forests, open grasslands, and serene riverine ecosystems. Visitors to Nagarahole National Park can embark on thrilling wildlife safaris, offering the chance to encounter majestic creatures such as elephants, tigers, leopards, and an array of bird species in their natural habitat. Beyond its wildlife, Nagarahole captivates with its tranquil beauty, pristine landscapes, and the opportunity for visitors to connect with nature in its purest form. Whether exploring the dense jungles, cruising along the riverbanks, or simply immersing oneself in the serenity of the surroundings, a visit to Nagarahole promises an unforgettable journey into the heart of the wilderness, leaving visitors enchanted by its timeless allure.

River Rafting

River rafting in Dubare offers an exhilarating adventure amidst the pristine beauty of Karnataka's wilderness. Located along the banks of the Cauvery River, Dubare presents a thrilling opportunity for enthusiasts to navigate through its turbulent waters, surrounded by lush forests and picturesque landscapes. As participants paddle through the rapids, they are treated to an adrenaline-pumping experience, complemented by the soothing melody of the flowing river. Dubare's rafting expeditions cater to all levels of expertise, from beginners seeking an introduction to the sport to seasoned adventurers craving a challenge. Safety measures are diligently enforced, ensuring a secure yet thrilling voyage down the river. Beyond the adrenaline rush, rafters are also immersed in the region's natural splendor, encountering diverse flora and fauna along the way. River rafting in Dubare promises an unforgettable escapade, blending excitement, serenity, and the raw beauty of nature into a seamless adventure.

Hiking

Embark on an enchanting hiking adventure amidst the lush landscapes of Coorg, where every step unveils a new facet of natural beauty. Traverse winding trails that meander through verdant forests, adorned with vibrant flora and echoing with the melodies of native birds. Ascend gentle slopes and rugged terrain to reach panoramic viewpoints that offer breathtaking vistas of mist-clad mountains and cascading waterfalls. Whether you're an avid trekker or a novice explorer, Coorg offers trails catering to every skill level, from leisurely strolls to challenging climbs. Along the way, encounter quaint villages, serene streams, and hidden gems waiting to be discovered. With each hike, immerse yourself in the tranquility of nature and forge unforgettable memories amidst the captivating landscapes of Coorg.

Encounter majestic elephants, graceful deer, and elusive big cats in their natural habitat, all while soaking in the breathtaking landscapes that define Coorg. Whether you're a wildlife enthusiast or simply seeking an adrenaline-filled escapade, a safari in Coorg promises an exhilarating journey through nature's playground.
